Food Facts


Aside from wanting people to e n j o y our meals at their own convenience, we also want to share our culture to those interested in it as our cuisinses reflect the influences of many countries. A Filipino meal is more inviting when all the food is served at once. In many ways, this is how Filipino food reflects Filipino culture. When multiple dishes are served at the same time, it is common for contrasting flavors to be served next to each other.


Adobo

The word adobo is derived from the Spanish word adobar, which means “marinade” or “pickling sauce.”

Kare-Kare

Kare-kare is a Philippine stew (kare derives from "curry") that features a thick savory peanut sauce.


Sinigang

The reason why it is called “sinigang” is that it was a translation to the English word, “stew.”

Bulalo

Bulalo is native to the Southern Luzon region, particularly in the provinces of Batangas and Cavite.
